Project Evolution

On March 28, 2025, our project entitled Études scientifiques sur la connectivité – Synthèse des connaissances was officially approved and funded by the Fonds de recherche du Québec – Nature et technologies (FRQNT), in partnership with the Secrétariat à l’Internet haute vitesse et aux projets spéciaux de connectivité (SIHV).

The project received a total funding envelope of $28,499 (including $22,440 for direct research and $6,059 for indirect research costs), with a project duration from April 1 to August 1, 2025.

Our synthesis focuses on the following two priority axes: Technological and Social Impacts. These were selected to reflect Quebec’s growing need for secure, sustainable, and inclusive connectivity infrastructures.

The research team brings together interdisciplinary expertise in edge/cloud computing, digital governance, AI-enabled connectivity strategies, and public policy analysis. This cross-domain integration aligns closely with the program’s mandate of bridging academic insight and government policy-making.

The project was ranked first among three submissions reviewed by the evaluation committee, with the following score breakdown:

  • Adequacy and contribution of the knowledge synthesis (70 pts): 68.33
  • Scientific quality and team training (20 pts): 17.67
  • Knowledge mobilization (10 pts): 9
  • Total: 95 / 100

The final deliverable will be a comprehensive, up-to-date synthesis submitted within four months, including a literature review, research gap identification, and actionable insights for SIHV’s advisory role to the Quebec government.
